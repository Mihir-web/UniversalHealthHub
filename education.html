Start your entrepreneurial journey

  • Work as a Foot Care Nurse visiting homes, retirement facilities, and long-term care centres.
  • By joining us, you are guaranteed a steady stream of clients, whether you work with us directly, at a retirement facility, a long-term care center, or a home care agency.
  • Foot care nurses typically charge between $75 to $80 per session, with seniors requiring follow-up appointments every 6 to 8 weeks.
  • Completing 50 foot care sessions each month can generate an additional $4,000 or more in income, supplementing your regular job as an RPN or RN.

CNO Registration Number
All Licensed Practical Nurses (LPNs), Registered Practical Nurses (RPNs) and Registered Nurses (RNs) that are in good standing with CNO College of Nurses of Ontario are eligible to apply for this certificate program.
Photo ID
Applicants must submit a valid photo identification document. Acceptable forms of ID include a Driverâs License, Passport, or Permanent Resident (PR) card.
Proof of Liability Insurance
Nurses need to provide proof of liability insurance, which can often be demonstrated through membership with professional organizations such as the WeRPN (Registered Practical Nurses Association of Ontario) or the RNAO (Registered Nurses' Association of Ontario). This insurance is essential for protecting the nurse against potential legal claims.
Proof of Covid-19 vaccination
While optional, submitting proof of Covid-19 vaccination is encouraged.
Proof of other vaccinations
Applicants must provide proof of vaccination for Hepatitis B and a negative TB test result.
Criminal reference check
A mandatory requirement, this check ensures that the applicant has a clear criminal record, particularly in relation to working with vulnerable populations.
